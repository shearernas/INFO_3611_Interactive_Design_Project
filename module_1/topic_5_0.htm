Topic 5: Getting familiar with the Terminal and the MySQL command line

MySQL does not work within a regular window, like your web browser or productivity software might.

Instead, you must use the "command line interface" to log into MySQL and use it. The "command line interface" (CLI) is like a very "barebones" way of interacting with your operating system.

It allows you to type in commands that tell your computer directly what it should do. It might seem super intimidating because it isn't a very "visual" way of interacting with your computer, but just to log into MySQL, it doesn't need much input!

On macOS, you'll want to open up the "Terminal" application. This can be found in your Launchpad, located on your Dock at the bottom of your screen. The icon looks like this.
